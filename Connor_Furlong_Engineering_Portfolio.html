About

I am a senior Mechanical Engineering student at the Milwaukee School of Engineering (MSOE) with a strong interest in energy-focused disciplines such as heat transfer, thermodynamics, and fluid mechanics. In addition to my coursework, I actively serve as Treasurer of the High Powered Rocketry Club and President of Club Soccer, roles that have honed my organizational and communication skills while keeping me engaged in hands-on, team-oriented projects.

This school year for my senior design project, I am developing a fuel injector for a turbojet engine and utilizing ANSYS Fluent to model and optimize performance, as well as SOLIDWORKS to design a working prototype. This work will deepen my experience in analysis, design, and simulation for high-energy, high-precision systems.

Most recently, I worked as a Product Development Engineering Intern at Rockwell Automation in Milwaukee, where I contributed to the design and testing of new products, collaborating with multidisciplinary teams to solve complex engineering challenges. Previously, as an Automation Engineering Intern at Rockwell, I focused on improving automated systems and processes, learning to adapt quickly and solve problems creatively. Earlier in my career, I served as a Rail Structure Intern at STV in Chicago, gaining experience in structural design and technical documentation.
